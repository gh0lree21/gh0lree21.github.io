Genesis Site Plan

Grant Holley

WDD 130-02

Overview

Purpose

I am a software engineering student. I am currently building a portfolio that I am going to be presenting to future employers. My website will contain a showcase of the work I have accomplished both on my own and in teams. When I apply for a job or graduate school my goal is to be able to say, “Here’s my portfolio website. I took part in the creation of everything on it, including the website itself.” You want to see what I can do? Go to my “Genesis” website and see how I have grown, that I am proud of my work, I’m diversified, and I love it when a plan comes together.

Audience

The umbrella audience for this website will be anyone who I will want to showcase my work for. The two main audiences, however consist of future employers and graduate schools. It is a tall order when I consider what this website could become for me because of this audience. They will want to see organization, diversity, and purpose. This will need to be easy to navigate and will need to contain concise details about different projects I have completed. It will need to be formal but still show my personality. The audience will be viewing through a professional lense and they will want to know the details quickly.

Purpose

'Hi, if you're here looking for Grant Holley, you've come to the right place. I've created this site as a place that you can get to know me better. So take a seat, find your reading glasses, and let's get started.' The obvious action that I will want people to do as a result of this is to choose me for the job, or school or homie. Is there some way that I could create an action that someone could complete while on the website that would get them already conceding to choosing me? Maybe a button on the home page could have wording like “Choose Me!” lol Likelihood of Use - To be honest, I will likely have a portfolio put together on another platform that I will submit with this website. An employer may choose to use the website over a folder because of a hyperlink perhaps. If I structure the website correctly, it may even be easier to view my work.

Buttons

Have a button or two that lead to other pages on the website. Perhaps the call to action for the home page could be a button to the bio or the employer section? I’m going to want multiple buttons in multiple plays to allow people to easy navigate between the different pages of the site. I in no way want them to feel trapped on a page. That could be a way of portraying an ‘open book’ feeling.

Employers

Who am I? (as a potential employee)

People will be asking, ‘Who is Grant Holley?’ ‘Why does he think I should hire him?’ ‘Does he meet the criteria for the job he is applying for?’ Critical thinkers will come to my site with industry knowledge and immediately begin seeking answers to how I code, if I’m adaptable, etc. This would be my first impression. My virtual handshake as it were (I may want to take an idea or two from Handshake as I organize data about myself).

Strategies for Selling Myself

So how do I make it easy to choose me? Contact info? I don’t love that idea, but it does alright for sure. The website also needs to be general enough that I don’t need to go in and adjust it for every potential employer who views it, so I need to be well represented, concise and I need to keep information relevant to different pages clearly separate so that people who go to the site can easily get exactly the information they are looking for. I could find some of the things that define me as a person and try to find ways to convey that in the website.

External Files and Links

Primarily the visitors to my site will be going to external files or sites to view projects in my portfolio. As such, the links that I use will need to be “the point” of the page and I will want to make them obvious. At the same time, if people feel like they have to navigate through a maze to get to the information they want, that will turn them off as well. I will want to supply as much information as possible on the website itself.

Fun Personal Info

Ultimately, the site is going to be a reflection on me. People will be asking, ‘Who is Grant Holley?’ I want to give them an opportunity to get some of my personality, but I don’t want to bog down the official introduction of myself either. Having a page dedicated to the fun side of myself would give people who visit an opportunity to learn some more about me, but only if they want. It would hopefully convey the fact that I can be business and I can be fun.

Personal Info

There is going to be some pretty personal information on the site about myself. I will likely include diplomas and other details (speaking of which I should try to include my associate’s degree diploma). I will also have to be careful what I put on there… to make it personal but since it will be a public website, to not give out any information that could be damaging.
